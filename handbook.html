  1. Notice of fraudulent product sales

2.1 What is prepaid fee fraud?

Advance fee fraud is a scheme in which the victim pays before receiving something of value. This type of fraud can be done through fake websites, fake e-mails claiming to be from the company, or fake social media profiles. Scammers require "a deposit" to be deposited before a transaction can be completed. Victims never receive their product.

For individuals in the United States, the following link may be helpful in further protecting themselves: https://www.fbi.gov/scams-and-safety/common-scams-and-crimes/advance-fee-schemes. (Heart Of Nature is not responsible for the content of this website.) For individuals outside the United States, check with your local government for resources to report and; or obtain extra protection.

We know of individuals who claim to be Heart Of Nature employees and offer our products for sale at substantial discounts. These individuals are not employees or in any way affiliated with Heart Of Nature or authorized to conduct business on behalf of Heart Of Nature. To make their plans seem authentic, Heart Of Nature's logo and business address using on fictitious contracts, email accounts create to mimic Heart Of Nature's official communications, and individuals may provide fraudulent identification documents such as passports. Heart Of Nature is not responsible for fraudulent contracts, agreements, or lost money refunds.

2.2. How to protect yourself from advance fee fraud?

All business activities of HON Company contact by phone number, email, and legal representative of the company. The detail provided by us on the HON website. Visit http://heartofnature.us/contact-us to make sure you have the correct information.

Websites and social media promoting discounted merchandise should be approached with caution. Individuals claiming to sell these products, typically require a small percentage of orders to be sent to them normally via bank transfer. Once the money is sent, the money is usually not recoverable.

Heart Of Nature generally does not use private messaging tools like WhatsApp, Gmail, Facebook, Facebook Messenger, or any other website to conduct business.

If you find Heart Of Nature products on a Heart Of Nature website that is not on our listings, that site is not legitimate.

If you believe you have been the victim of a fraudulent sales arrangement, please provide information to us at http://heartofnature.us/contact-us

  1. Notification of fraudulent job offer

2.1. What is recruitment fraud?

Recruitment fraud is a sophisticated scam that provides fake job opportunities to job seekers. This type of fraud can do through fake websites, fake e-mails claiming to be from the company or fake social media profiles. Scammers often ask recipients for personal information and payment as part of their counterfeit recruitment process.

For individuals in the United States, the following link may be helpful in further protecting themselves: www.consumer.ftc.gov/articles/0243-job-scams. (Heart Of Nature is not responsible for the content of this website.) For individuals outside of the United States, check with your local government for resources to report and; or obtain extra protection.

We are aware that job postings made by people claiming to be Heart Of Nature recruiters, even using Heart Of Nature employee names, but not affiliated with Heart Of Nature. We have also informed that unauthorized individuals may have been posing as Heart Of Nature recruiters, contacting about job opportunities, and extending job offers via text message, instant messages, or chat rooms. These job postings and job offers do not authorize by Heart Of Nature. Heart Of Nature is not responsible for fraudulent offers or claims for personal information.

2.2. How to protect yourself from recruitment fraud

We use the company's legitimate phone number and email to communicate with candidates about the company's employment information. You can visit our website http://heartofnature.us/contact-us to run with us, learn about jobs and apply.

Be wary of employers asking candidates to submit unusual personal information like bank statements or utility bills, pay a fee to be considered for a job, or pay "startup expenses" after being recruited. Heart Of Nature does not use private messaging tools such as Google Hangouts, Gmail, Facebook, Facebook Messenger, or other similar tools to extend job offers.

If you find a Heart Of Nature post on a site other than ours, verify the legitimacy of that site before applying by visiting our site and finding out.

If you believe you are a victim of recruitment fraud, please share your information with us at http://heartofnature.us/contact-us. Thanks for your interest in working at Heart Of Nature.
